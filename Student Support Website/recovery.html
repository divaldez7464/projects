BASICS ABOUT SLEEP

1. How Much Sleep Do I Need?

How much sleep you need changes as you age.

College students fall into the category of Teen or Adults. This age gap ranges from 13-60 years old. Per 24 hours, 7 hours to 10 hours is recommended for the amount of sleep for a person to perform their best.

Although the amount of sleep you get each day is important, other aspects of your sleep also contribute to your health and well-being. Good sleep quality is also essential. Signs of poor sleep quality include not feeling rested even after getting enough sleep, repeatedly waking up during the night, and experiencing symptoms of sleep disorders (such as snoring or gasping for air). Improving sleep quality may be helped by better sleep habits or being diagnosed and treated for any sleep disorder you may have.

2. Tips for Better Sleep

  • Be consistent. Go to bed at the same time each night and get up at the same time each morning, including on the weekends
  • Make sure your bedroom is quiet, dark, relaxing, and at a comfortable temperature
  • Remove electronic devices, such as TVs, computers, and smart phones, from the bedroom
  • Avoid large meals, caffeine, and alcohol before bedtime
  • Get some exercise. Being physically active during the day can help you fall asleep more easily at night.

4. Habits To Try To Incorporate Into Sleep Schedule

There are some small ways to try to help you sleep and can be included into your nighly routine. Depending on the sleepers preference, the sound of a fan or air humidifier can help ease a person into sleep, but some may prefer silence and can only sleep if it is quiet. Another habit to break is looking at your phone a couple hours before you go to sleep. The strain on your eyes from the blue light will make it hard to rest your eyes even when they feel tired. Instead of your phone, one could try to read a book to help relax the eyes and mind. Lastly, ASMR (autonomous sensory meridian response) can be watched or listened to. These sounds can trigger tingling or goosebump sensation, which helps relax the mind.
